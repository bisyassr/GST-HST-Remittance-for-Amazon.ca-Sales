An Interactive Explainer for the Canada Revenue Agency

Executive Summary

This interactive report demonstrates that Amazon, operating as a designated Marketplace Facilitator, legally assumes the responsibility for calculating, collecting, and remitting GST/HST on certain domestic sales. This is a direct result of Canadian tax legislation effective **July 1, 2021**. Consequently, tax amounts for these specific transactions were remitted directly by Amazon to the CRA and were not included in the seller's payouts. The seller bears no further remittance obligation for these amounts.

Legislative Timeline for Digital Economy Taxation

Amazon's role as a Marketplace Facilitator is a direct response to specific legislative changes by the CRA. This timeline highlights the key effective dates.

July 1, 2021

New GST/HST legislative measures for digital economy businesses and platform operators become effective. This is the foundational change that legally defines Amazon's role as a "Distribution Platform Operator" and mandates its responsibility for tax collection and remittance in specific scenarios.

April 1, 2021

British Columbia begins requiring non-resident vendors to collect PST on cross-border digital services, signaling a broader trend in provincial tax modernization that parallels federal efforts.

January 1, 2024

New rules for GST/HST on short-term rentals provided through digital platforms take effect, further solidifying the legal framework for marketplace facilitators in Canada's digital economy.

Verification Through Amazon Seller Central

Amazon provides specific reports to identify transactions where it has withheld and remitted tax. This allows for a clear and verifiable audit trail.

Order Details & Payments Reporting

This is the most direct place to see the tax deduction on a per-order basis.

  • Location: Seller Central > Orders > Manage Orders / Reports > Payments
  • Key Identifier: Look for line items labeled "Marketplace Withheld Tax" or "Marketplace Facilitator Tax".
  • Meaning: These labels explicitly represent the MTC amounts deducted from an order, confirming Amazon's obligation to remit. The funds are withheld from the seller's payout for this purpose.

GST/HST Tax Withholding Report

A summary report specifically designed for tracking tax withheld by Amazon.

  • Location: Seller Central > Reports > Tax Documents > Tax Withheld By Amazon
  • Key Identifier: The report itself is titled "GST/HST Tax Withholding report".
  • Meaning: Provides a summary for a specified date range of total sales, taxable sales, GST collected, and the total tax withheld by Amazon for direct remittance.

Amazon Transaction Report

The most granular report for detailed reconciliation of all financial activity.

  • Location: Seller Central > Reports > Fulfillment > Sales reports
  • Key Identifier: Within the report columns, tax collected is itemized, explicitly distinguishing between MTC (Marketplace Facilitator) and seller-collected amounts.
  • Meaning: This detailed, transaction-level data allows for precise reconciliation, separating sales where Amazon remitted tax from sales where the seller retains the remittance responsibility.

Conclusion & Recommendation

The evidence confirms a clear shift in tax liability for specific transactions from the seller to the marketplace platform, as mandated by Canadian law.

Recommendation to the Canada Revenue Agency

The CRA is respectfully requested to acknowledge that for all transactions identified in Amazon's reports with a "Marketplace Withheld Tax" deduction, the GST/HST has been duly collected and remitted by Amazon as the designated Marketplace Facilitator. The seller has fulfilled their obligation for these amounts and should not be required to remit them again. Verification for these transactions should be directed towards Amazon's remittance records.
